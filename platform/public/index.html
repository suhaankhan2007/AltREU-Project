Field guide

How to read a light curve

  • Time (x) Each dot is a single night's observation, moving left to right.
  • Brightness (y) Up means brighter. You're looking for a temporary spike in starlight, which looks like a single hill or hump.
  • Baseline The star's normal, everyday brightness before anything interesting happens.

Astronomers use magnitudes to measure brightness, where a smaller number actually means a brighter star. To save you a headache, we flipped the chart so up always means brighter.

Microlensing

  • A smooth, single hump. The star gets steadily brighter, peaks, and fades right back to normal. This is exactly what we're hunting for! Most events last 10 to 40 days.

Variable star

  • A repeating rollercoaster. The star pulses or eclipses regularly, making a continuous wave pattern rather than one isolated peak.

Noise

  • Messy, random scatter with no clear shape. Mark it as noise and move on.
